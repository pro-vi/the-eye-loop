### Minimum number of binary decisions to converge

There is no single universal number because it depends on (a) the hypothesis class, (b) noise, (c) the desired precision, and (d) the intrinsic dimension/sparsity of preferences. But you can anchor the discussion with three robust results families:

- **Information-theoretic lower bound (finite hypotheses)**: if there are \(N\) discrete possibilities and answers are noiseless, you need at least \(\log_2 N\) bits, i.e., \(\Omega(\log_2 N)\) binary decisions.
